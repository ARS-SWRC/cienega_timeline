Citations within the timeline column for source will follow the Council of Science Editors standards except that the journal titles will be entered in full and we will omit the extent for books. This means that existing citations/sources need to be updated per this format. This form will help create the necessary format for the Cienega Watershed Timeline events based on the Council of Science Editors citation style. (https://writing.wisc.edu/Handbook/DocCSE.html)
An example of the citation style used for the Cienega Watershed Timeline Project: ** Book: References for books follow the order Author(s). Title. Edition. Place of publication: publisher; date.
Allen C, Prior P, Hayward AC. Bacterial wilt: the disease and the Ralstonia solanacearum species complex. St. Paul (MN): APS Press; 2005. ** Chapter in a book or Paper in Symposium Proceedings: Author(s). Chapter title. In: Editor(s). Book title. Place of publication: publisher; year. Page numbers for that chapter.
Otegui MS. Endosperm: development and molecular biology. In: Olson OA, editor. Endosperm cell walls: formation, composition, and functions. Heidelberg (Germany): Springer-Verlag; 2007. p. 159-178. ** Journal article: Author(s). Article title. Full journal title. Date;volume(issue):pages.
Wang Y, Zhu J, DeLuca HF. Where is the vitamin D receptor? Archives of Biochemistry and Biophysics. 2012 Jul 1;523(1):123-33.
